What is SushiSwap?

SushiSwap is Sushi's decentralized exchange and DeFi app for token swaps, AMM liquidity pools, and aggregator routing. The official Sushi docs describe the protocol as a DeFi platform built around an AMM, aggregator, SUSHI governance, and related products.

How SushiSwap works

  1. Open the verified appUse the official sushi.com app route before connecting a wallet or approving any token.
  2. Connect your walletSelect the wallet and network you want to use, then confirm you are on the intended chain.
  3. Choose the pairEnter the token you want to swap from and the token you want to receive, then review the quote.
  4. Review and signCheck token addresses, route, slippage, gas, and output before confirming in your wallet.

Before you swap

Aggregator routing

Sushi's router can compare available liquidity across decentralized exchanges, a product area covered in the official Sushi docs.

AMM liquidity

The AMM uses liquidity pools instead of an order book, allowing token swaps through on-chain pool contracts.

Self-custody flow

You connect a wallet and sign transactions yourself; ethereum.org's DeFi overview explains the broader model.

SushiSwap FAQ

Is sushiswap the official app?

The official Sushi web app is listed in the official resources and routes on www.sushi.com; verify that domain before connecting a wallet.

What does sushiswap let me swap?

It supports token swaps on supported networks through Sushi's app; availability depends on wallet network, token liquidity, and current routing.

Do I need a wallet for sushiswap?

Yes. Swaps are wallet-confirmed transactions, so review token addresses, approvals, network, gas, and quoted output before signing.

Is sushiswap custodial?

No. The app is designed for self-custody wallet use; smart contract and token risks still apply, so verify each transaction.

Can sushiswap guarantee the best price?

No. Aggregator routing can compare available liquidity, but execution can change with liquidity, slippage, gas, and market movement.
